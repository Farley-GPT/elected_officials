Mayor of Portland

I am a father, a neighbor, and a proud Portlander with a vision for a city crying out for real change.

We all see the pain of our unsheltered neighbors, the devastating impact on our community, and the unsustainable strain on local government. Enough is enough. We refuse to accept the status quo. Within one year, I’ll end unsheltered homelessness in our city.

You’ve heard promises before. Here’s the hard-won experience that will make this a reality:

In addition to my decades as an executive, I am also the founder of Shelter Portland, an innovative non-profit organization making progress in the battle to end unsheltered homelessness. I have assembled a team of experts with a winning blueprint to compassionately and immediately eliminate unsheltered homelessness without further burdening our taxpayers. We’ve seen real results, but we have so much more work to do.

Reforming our approach to unsheltered homelessness is a big challenge, but it isn’t my first. My transformational leadership took a decades-old trucking business, eliminated all fossil fuel usage in Portland, and built an unmatched safety culture while growing the company six times over. I want to bring that same award-winning innovation and operational competence to the city we love.

The city politicians and their failed policies that got us into this mess can’t be trusted to get us out. We need real change for our great city.

I’m rolling up my sleeves to get to work, and I want you by my side. Let’s build Portland’s future together.

Keith Wilson

City Auditor

Simone Rede

City Auditor

Simone Rede Re-Elected as Portland City Auditor

Simone Rede was re-elected as Portland City Auditor in the 2024 election, continuing her tenure that began in January 2023. As the first person of color and the first LGBTQ+ individual to hold this position, Rede has brought a fresh perspective to the role of Portland's elected, independent watchdog.

A Background in Public Service

Rede's professional background is deeply rooted in public service and auditing. She is a Certified Internal Auditor and a Certified Government Auditing Professional with over 15 years of experience in public service. Her academic credentials include a master's degree in public policy and management from Carnegie Mellon University and a bachelor's degree in urban studies from Vassar College.

Prior to becoming City Auditor, Rede worked as a principal management auditor at Metro regional government and as a staff auditor at the Oregon Secretary of State Audits Division. Her experience extends to improving access to higher education in Portland area alternative schools. Rede's expertise has been recognized nationally, as she serves on the United States Government Accountability Office Domestic Working Group, addressing accountability issues across all levels of government.

A Focus on Accountability and Accessibility

During her first term, Rede focused on making Portland's city government more accountable and accessible to the public. She implemented several initiatives, including building a constituent relations function for the Office and releasing reports that highlighted mismanagement, waste, and harm to Portlanders. Notably, she oversaw the first City audit of the Joint Office of Homeless Services.

Rede's approach to auditing is people-centered, emphasizing the selection of topics that matter to Portlanders and inviting public input to inform the work of her office. She has actively sought community perspectives through surveys and focus groups when developing audit schedules and gathering evidence for conclusions.

Personal Life and Continued Service

In her personal life, Rede is an Oregonian, born in Salem and raised in Portland. She describes herself as the daughter of journalists, which she credits for her questioning nature. Rede is also an artist, having sung in choirs and played musical instruments for most of her life. She lives in northeast Portland with her wife and their three dogs.

As Portland transitions to a new form of government, Rede's re-election ensures continuity and stability. Her experience and leadership are considered assets to the City and the people of Portland during this period of change. Rede's commitment to protecting the Office's independence and educating newly elected and appointed leaders about the Auditor's role remains a top priority for her second term.

Portland's New Form of Government

2024 Charter Reform Implementation

Click to learn more

📜

Portland's new form of government represents the most significant change to the city's governance structure in over a century. Key changes include:

  • Four geographic districts with three council members each
  • Professional city administrator to manage daily operations
  • Mayor focused on citywide leadership and policy
  • City Council concentrated on legislative and oversight duties
  • Ranked choice voting for all city elections

Ranked Choice Voting

Understanding Portland's New Voting System

Click to learn more

🗳️

Ranked choice voting allows voters to rank candidates in order of preference. Here's how it works:

  • Voters rank candidates in order of preference (1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.)
  • If no candidate receives over 50% of first-choice votes, the candidate with the fewest votes is eliminated
  • Votes for the eliminated candidate are redistributed to voters' next choices
  • Process continues until a candidate receives a majority
  • This system ensures broader representation and eliminates the need for runoff elections
